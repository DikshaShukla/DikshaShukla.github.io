Research

My research interest revolves around the security of smart devices using advanced machine learning techniques, video analysis, and statistical analysis. The use of smart devices such as smartphones, smartwatches, smart glasses, etc., became a part of everyday life. The usage of these devices is now not limited to simple phone calls but ranges from private text messages to very crucial banking transactions. Since people store very confidential information on their smart devices, it has become increasingly important to build and ensure the security of these systems. Also, an important step to secure a system is to identify the security vulnerabilities in the concerned system.

My PhD work in the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at Syracuse University was on Inferences from Interactions with Smart Devices with focus on Security Leaks and Defenses.

I focus specifically in these areas:

  • Machine Learning
  • Reinforcement Learning
  • Computer Vision
  • Cybersecurity
  • Biometrics
